Heian Period (794-1185)
A transformative era in Japanese history marked by the establishment of a new capital, the flourishing of classical culture, and the gradual rise of the samurai warrior class.

üìÖ Timeline

794: Establishment

Emperor Kanmu moves the capital to Heian-ky≈ç (modern-day Kyoto). This marks the beginning of the Heian period and the end of Nara period rule.

9th-10th Century: Fujiwara Regency

The Fujiwara clan rises to prominence, controlling the imperial court through regency positions. This leads to a golden age of arts, literature, and architecture.

11th Century: Cultural Peak

Production of masterpieces like "The Tale of Genji" by Murasaki Shikibu. Classic literature, poetry, and aesthetics reach their zenith.

1156: H≈çgen Rebellion

First major military conflict between samurai clans, marking the beginning of the end of imperial court power and the rise of the warrior class.

1185: Genpei War Ends

Climax at Dan-no-ura naval battle. The Minamoto clan defeats the Taira clan, establishing the shogunate system and ending the Heian period.
